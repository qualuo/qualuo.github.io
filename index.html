Principles

Occam's Razor
I believe that the simpler explanation is better. Designing for simplicity is preferred, so I keep things as simple as possible.

Dunnig-Kruger Effect
This effect is the bias in a person with low ability assessing themselves as superior. In the same vein as "The more you know, the more you know you don't know," said by Aristotle, and Einstein's “The more I learn, the more I realize how much I don't know.” I understand that there is so much more than one can possibly know, and thus avoid overrating my abilities. A person can not accurately evaluate oneself. Only when compared to others can that be done.

Not Reinventing the Wheel
Effort is wasted when making something that already exists. That effort should instead be used to solve new problems, or at least make improved solutions to make progress. After all, we stand on the shoulders of giants.
