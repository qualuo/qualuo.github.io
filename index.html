Principles

Occam's Razor
I believe that the simpler explanation is (usually) better. Designing for simplicity is preferred. Explaining things in a simple manner keeps things understandable and clear with less expended mental capacity, so I like to keep things as simple as possible.

Dunnig-Kruger Effect
This effect is the bias in a person with low ability assessing themself as superior. In the same vein as "The more you know, the more you know you don't know," said by Aristotle, and Einstein's quote: “The more I learn, the more I realize how much I don't know.” I understand that there is so much more than one can possibly know, and thus avoid overrating my abilities. A person can not accurately evaluate oneself. Only when compared to others can that be done.

Not Reinventing the Wheel
Effort is wasted when recreating something that already exists. That effort should instead be used to solve new problems, or at least make improved solutions to make progress. After all, we stand on the shoulders of giants.

Honesty
I am a very honest person. I believe in a world without lies because it would be far more straight forward. Deception is a hindrance to improvement and progress.

Optimism
I look at the bright things and try to avoid negativity. I believe in the greatness of humanity. Evident in our technological progress and ingenuity. Those who believe in the opposite would say that humanity is a plague to the world. I believe the opposite of that. I believe that whatever happens to the world, we will strive to look for sustainable methods and adjust to new conditions, and move towards a brighter future. So that, ultimately, we may find the answers to our unanswered questions.
