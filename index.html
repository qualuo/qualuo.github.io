+ Principles that I live by

Occam's Razor
I believe that the simpler explanation is better. Designing for simplicity is prefered. I keep things as simple as possible. Related to "Keep it simple, stupid."

Dunnig-Kruger effect
Is the bias of in a person with low ability assessing themselves as superior. I also agree with the saying "The more you know, the more you know you don't know" said by Aristotle, similar to Einstein's “The more I learn, the more I realize how much I don't know.” I understand that there is so much more than one can possibly know, and thus avoid overrating my abilities. A person can not accurately evaluate one self. Only when compared to others can that be done.

Optimism
I look at the bright things and try to avoid negativity. I believe in the greatness of humanity, evident by our progress. Evident in our advanced technology and intellect. Those who believe in the opposite would say that humanity is a plague. I believe that whatever happens, we will strive to look for sustainable methods and adjust to new conditions.

Honesty
